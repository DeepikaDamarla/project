Shillong
Shillong, the "Scotland of the East," boasts lush green hills, cascading waterfalls, and a vibrant cultural tapestry, all nestled in the serene landscapes of Meghalaya, India. Its charm lies in the fusion of colonial architecture, Khasi traditions, and breathtaking natural beauty.

Cherrapunji
Cherrapunji, located in India's northeastern state of Meghalaya, is renowned as one of the wettest places on Earth, boasting lush greenery and breathtaking waterfalls amidst its dramatic landscapes. Its annual rainfall record draws travelers seeking a glimpse of its misty valleys and vibrant flora.

Mawsynram
Mawsynram, located in Meghalaya, India, is renowned as the wettest place on Earth, with annual rainfall averaging around 467 inches. Its lush green landscapes and cascading waterfalls draw visitors seeking the awe-inspiring beauty of its rain-soaked terrain.
